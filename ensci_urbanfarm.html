How to encourage citizens to get involved in urban farming?

FERME URBAINE DE SAINT DENIS

2019

context

I worked on this project for 6 months with 3 other team members as part of the master “Innovation by Design” at ENSCI Les Ateliers (Ecole Nationale Supérieure de Création Industrielle - French national industrial design school). The project was for the group Les Fermes de Gally (Gally farms) which had recently acquired a urban farm in the city of Saint-Denis (suburbs of Paris).

the project

The challenge for the new “Ferme Urbaine de Saint-Denis” was to find ways to be more attractive to citizens and build awareness about nature in cities.
Immersion : observations in different areas of the city of Saint-Denis and in the surroundings of the urban farm (presence of “green” areas, urban furniture, number/age/gender of people…)
User research : interviews with 15 people (different age groups and gender) to understand their relation to the city, their approach to nature as urban citizens. We created a map “You and Saint-Denis” and for each interview, we asked interviewees to draw their daily itineraries, favorite and inevitable places... to understand how they “live” the city.
Prototyping & testing : based on the insights of the user research and on inspiration from different sectors, we prototyped a game we called “Open Gardens” to incite citizens via “step by step” challenges to get involved in the “vegetalization” of the city at different levels : home, street, neighborhood, city. We tested the concept by interviewing habitants and showing them a storyboard and mockup of the game cards.

personal learnings

User research skills (immersion, interviews, observations), prototyping & testing, challenges for urban green places
