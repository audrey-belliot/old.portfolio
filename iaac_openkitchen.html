Open kitchen - Can we cook in the streets?

auto-ethnographic design intervention

2021

context

Exploring a topic in a "first person perspective" / auto-ethnographic research
Master in Design for Emergent Futures at IAAC x Elisava x FabLab BCN

the project

Interested by public spaces and food accessibility, I always wanted to experiment with the concept of public outdoor community kitchens. I see it as a way to enable homeless people to have a space to cook // break the top-down dynamic of food emergency distribution // create new social ties between random people by sharing the moment of cooking.

I cooked and ate in the streets of Barcelona during one day.

Take back the street? Hack the public space? This one day experiment made it clear that if I was feeling uncomfortable, it was largely due to the fact that no place in the city really allows me to do that, most of the space allows me to walk, to pass, but not to stay.
This experiment could be extended to more streets, public spaces and we could experiment eating there to map where it is appropriate or not and see how we "hack" it to make it possible.
